Chapter 02 — The Crises

Six ways we are
destroying ourselves

1.5° Climate tipping point

Beyond 1.5°C, irreversible feedback loops trigger. Melting permafrost releases methane, accelerating the warming we're trying to halt.

8M Tonnes of ocean plastic / year

By 2050, more plastic than fish by weight. Microplastics found in human blood, placentas, and Arctic ice cores.

46% Forest cover lost since prehistory

Half the world's trees gone since civilisation began. The Amazon alone loses 10,000 acres every single day.

69% Wildlife decline since 1970

Earth's sixth mass extinction event — the first driven by a single species. Biodiversity loss at 1,000× natural rates.

40% Coral reefs bleached

Rising ocean temperatures bleach reefs worldwide. The Great Barrier Reef has lost half its corals since 1995.

422 ppm CO₂ — highest in 3M years

Not this high since the Pliocene, when sea levels were 25 metres higher and the Arctic was forested.

Chapter 04 — How We Got Here

A timeline of
destruction

We are the first generation to feel the effect of climate change and the last generation that can do something about it.
— Barack Obama, 2014
1800
Industrial Revolution Begins
CO₂ at 280 ppm. Forests largely intact. Fossil fuel combustion begins at scale across Europe.
1950
The Great Acceleration
Post-war boom triggers exponential growth in resource consumption, plastic production, and deforestation rates.
1970
Biodiversity Baseline — collapse begins
WWF Living Planet Index begins tracking. Since this year, vertebrate populations have declined 69% on average.
1988
IPCC Founded — Warnings Ignored
Scientists formally warn governments. Oil companies simultaneously fund decades of denial campaigns.
2016
Great Barrier Reef Mass Bleaching
67% of shallow-water corals die in weeks. Entire interconnected ecosystems collapse simultaneously.
2024
Hottest Year Ever Recorded — Again
11th consecutive year of record-breaking temperatures. Arctic sea ice at historic lows. 1.5°C threshold breached.
Now
The Window Is Closing
Scientists give until 2030 to halve global emissions. Every fraction of a degree matters. Every action counts.

Chapter 02 — The Crises

Six ways we are
destroying ourselves

```
1.5° Climate tipping point

Beyond 1.5°C of warming, we trigger irreversible feedback loops — melting permafrost releases methane, accelerating the very warming we're trying to stop.

8M Tonnes of ocean plastic / year

By 2050, the ocean will contain more plastic by weight than fish. Microplastics have been found in human blood, placentas, and Arctic ice.

46% Forest cover lost since prehistory

Humans have cut down nearly half the world's trees since civilization began. The Amazon alone loses 10,000 acres every single day.

69% Wildlife decline since 1970

Earth is entering its sixth mass extinction event — the first driven by a single species. We are losing biodiversity 1,000x faster than natural background rates.

40% Coral reefs already bleached

Rising ocean temperatures are bleaching coral reefs worldwide. The Great Barrier Reef has lost half its corals since 1995. Once gone, they cannot return.

422 ppm CO₂ — highest in 3M years

Atmospheric CO₂ has not been this high since the Pliocene epoch, when sea levels were 25 meters higher and the Arctic was forested.
```

Chapter 04 — How We Got Here

A timeline of
destruction

1800
Industrial Revolution Begins
CO₂ concentrations at 280 ppm. Earth's forests largely intact. Fossil fuel burning begins at scale.
1950
The Great Acceleration
Post-war economic boom triggers exponential growth in resource consumption, plastic production, and deforestation.
1970
Biodiversity baseline — species begin their collapse
WWF Living Planet Index begins tracking. Since this year, vertebrate populations have declined by an average of 69%.
1988
IPCC Founded — warnings ignored
Scientists formally warn governments. Oil companies simultaneously fund decades of climate denial campaigns.
2016
Great Barrier Reef mass bleaching
67% of shallow-water corals die in the northern Great Barrier Reef. Entire ecosystems collapse in weeks.
2024
Hottest year ever recorded — again
For the 11th consecutive year, global temperature records broken. Arctic sea ice at historic lows. 1.5°C threshold breached.
Now
The window to act is closing
Scientists give us until 2030 to halve global emissions. Every fraction of a degree matters. Every action counts.
